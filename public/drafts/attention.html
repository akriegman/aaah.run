Attention Is All There Is

Attention is very important. I probably don’t need to convince you of this. But I claim that attention is even more important than you think it is, which I definitionally do need to convince you of. More specifically, I claim that attention is the most valuable resource on Earth, and that in some sense attention is more fundamental than “existence” and “entropy”.

What are the most successful species on Earth? It’s hard to compare ants to trees, and it’s not clear how to define “success”, but we can choose some reasonable definition and compare similar species, eg dogs to wolves, corn to rye, cows to deer, etc. If we do this, a pattern starts to emerge: the most successful species are the ones that have managed to harness human attention1.

Who are the largest consumers of attention? Advertisers is a good guess, but they’re second. The largest consumers of attention are employers.

Socialization

Most nonverbal communication and other hemming and hawing is just a way to barter for attention. You can express excitement while someone is talking to communicate to the group that you have something to say. The level of excitement you show indicates how worthy of attention the thing you want to say is. When multiple people have something to say, this nonverbal (well actually it is verbal, but not full sentences) communication is usually enough for everyone to know who’s thing is the most interesting, and everyone else steps back and lets them speak. At least, in my circles most people are good at this game and play cooperatively. Some people I meet are bad at this game and they are very annoying.

There’s a bit of a prisoner’s dilemma here. When Alice speaks, everyone else gains some value from hearing what she has to say, and Alice gains some respect and social capital. Bob has something slightly less interesting to say, but interesting enough that the social capital he would gain from speaking is of greater value to him than hearing what Alice has to say. Bob can express a dishonest level of excitement to make the group listen to him instead of Alice. But in practice, in my circles at least, people do not defect. Why? The answer is that we lose respect for people who defect. If you take up too much space in the conversation, then people are less likely to want to be around you. This is enough to offset the incentive for Bob to misrepresent what he has to say.

What is attention?

We don’t need a precise definition of attention for the above discussion to make sense, but I believe that there is a physically precise definition that will allow us to make substantial statements. It goes something like this: an observer system \(O\) is paying attention to a primary system \(A\) if the state of \(O\) depends on the state of \(A\). There’s some work to be done to make this physically precise and time dependent, and maybe to make it a quantity instead of just a relation. But this rough definition is good enough for speculation and applications.

Entropy

When physicists talk about the entropy of a physical system, it’s always conditioned on some high level constraints which are in general fuzzy. They’ll give a description of the system, then they’ll usually impose some numeric constraints such as an expected energy, which isn’t so bad, and then they’ll impose some further constraints like “the oxygen is all on this side of the membrane and the nitrogen is all on the other.” Then the entropy is the randomness left in the system subject to these constraints. The problem is that the entropy is then not a property of the system, it’s a property of the system plus these constraints, which can in general be anything we can describe.

I believe the way to resolve this is to embrace the subjectivity of entropy and make that subjectivity rigorous. We can choose some observer system \(O\) such as a thermometer, a person, or a neuron, and ask how much (Shannon) entropy is left in the primary system after conditioning on the state of the observer.

Existence

One corner case worth considering is when \(O\) is the entire universe. “The universe’s state depends on your state” is the same as saying you exist. So you exist if and only if the universe is paying attention to you. Attention from the universe is “natural selection”, and attention from other systems is other types of selection.

This is a trivial edge case, but there is a more substantial sense in which existence is attention. When you have sex with someone, you are paying them a certain type of attention with certain parts of your body, and this attention is necessary for their continued existence. When I say “their” continued existence, obviously as an individual they do not need sex for their continued existence, but we are not individuals2.

So this framework makes it precise how sexual selection and natural selection are two specific cases of the same general thing.

Intelligence

Choosing what to pay attention to is arguably the primary role of intelligent systems. Some of the primitive mechanisms of intelligence are ion channels in living things, neurons in the brain, transistors in computing, and ReLU-like activation functions in deep learning. What these all have in common is that their primary function is to select when information is propogated and when it is discarded. Evolution itself, the primitive intelligent system that all other intelligent systems are a part of, is driven by natural selection, a special case of attention. The “attention mechanism” in machine learning — the innovation behind the current language model revolution, famously popularized by the Google paper “Attention Is All You Need” — is an example of an “attention mechanism”.

Duality

Maybe I’m just a dualitypilled mathcel, but I think there’s some sort of duality between attention and information. Information is what a system puts out, attention is what it takes in, and there is a natural pairing between attention and information since information is what you pay attention to. There’s two main types of dualities in mathematics: the kind where the dual objects are equal and opposite to the primal objects (eg dual polyhedra, finite dimensional dual vector spaces), and the kind where the dual objects occupy a vaster space and have additional structure making them more powerful (eg algebraic geometry, infinite dimensional dual vector spaces). I think the attention-information duality may be the second type. Here are some of the asymmetries:

  • Information is easy to copy, making it much cheaper than attention which cannot be duplicated.
  • Information speaks for itself, while attention can only be understood through the information it pays attention to. This is why you can learn most of what there is to know about someone’s body within a few seconds of meeting them, but it takes years to get to know their mind.

IQ & SQ

I have a pet theory that intelligence has two main dimensions, corresponding to information and attention. I’m going to redefine IQ to be information quotient, your ability to produce “interesting” information. The other dimension is SQ, selection quotient, your ability to choose what is most interesting to pay attention to. I think that IQ as defined here may be closer to IQ in the traditional sense, or maybe not idrk. I’m also not sure if these two parts completely capture intelligence. For example think about how AlphaZero works. It has a policy network to decide what actions are worth considering, a value network to decide which positions are promising, and a search algorithm that uses these two networks to think ahead and find the best move. IQ is like the policy network, SQ is like the value network, and that search algorithm seems to lie outside of both.

I’m not putting forward any concrete conjectures and I have no idea what conjectures look like in psychology, this just feels like a compelling perspective to me. But the more I think about the world through this lens, the more it starts to feel somewhat explanatory. For example, autism is basically just when your SQ is low. An autistic person may notice that the way their foot hits the ground is slightly off, overthink this, and end up walking funny. They have payed too much attention to something unimportant and ended up worse off for it, bringing something into their conscious attention that does not belong there. This would also explain hyperfixation, sensory sensitivity, getting upset over small injustices, etc. This just feels like a simple and perfect description of what autism is.

Now I’m going to get really speculative. I’m not a neuroscientist, and a lot of what I’m about to say is probably obviously wrong to anyone who knows what’s going on. Sometimes in my dreams, there are plot points which just feel too clever to be random, and yet they don’t feel like they came from me, I feel surprised by them. It’s as if there’s an intelligence in my brain that is not part of my “self” or my mind. This intelligence creates ideas, and my mind receives them. It’s almost as if creating information and selecting that information are handled by two different subsystems of the brain, and we identify more strongly with the selection subsystem, as if it is the self, the mind inside the brain. They could even be physically seperate, in the sense that neurons are part of one or the other.

Speculating further, I think one of the main functions of this selection subsystem is focus, which is when it inhibits neurons that would get in the way of your current train of thought. And I feel like the effect of marijuanna is essentially to weaken this focus, reducing the inhibition of these neurons. This can make you more creative by unblocking pathways that are normally blocked, allowing you to consider ideas you normally wouldn’t consider, many of which you were right to not consider. It also makes your brain louder, making it harder to follow a train of thought because there’s all of this unimportant noise stealing your attention.
